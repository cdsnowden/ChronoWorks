ChronoWorks Privacy Policy

Last Updated: January 2025

1. Introduction

ChronoWorks ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our employee time tracking application and related services (collectively, the "Service").

Please read this privacy policy carefully. If you do not agree with the terms of this privacy policy, please do not access the Service.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide to Us

  • Account Information: When your employer registers your company or you create an account, we collect your name, email address, phone number, job title, and password.
  • Company Information: Business name, address, industry, number of employees, and contact information.
  • Profile Information: Profile pictures, employee ID, department, and other information you choose to provide.

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

  • Time Tracking Data: Clock in/out times, break times, shift schedules, time entries, and total hours worked.
  • Location Data: When you enable location services and your subscription plan includes GPS tracking, we collect precise geolocation data (latitude and longitude) when you clock in, clock out, start breaks, and end breaks. This helps verify that employees are at authorized work locations.
  • Device Information: Device type, operating system, unique device identifiers, IP address, and mobile network information.
  • Usage Data: Information about how you interact with the Service, including features used, pages viewed, and time spent in the app.
Important Note About Location Tracking: GPS location tracking is only available on Silver plan ($89.99/month) and higher. Location data is only collected when you actively clock in/out or start/end breaks. We do not track your location when you are not using the time clock feature. You must grant location permissions in your device settings for this feature to work. You can disable location permissions at any time, though this may affect certain features like geofencing.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Provide the Service: Enable time tracking, schedule management, payroll calculations, and other core features.
  • Verify Work Location: Use GPS data to confirm employees are at authorized work locations when clocking in/out (if enabled by your employer).
  • Calculate Compensation: Track hours worked, overtime, breaks, and time off for payroll purposes.
  • Send Notifications: Alert you about schedule changes, overtime warnings, time off approvals, and other work-related notifications.
  • Improve the Service: Analyze usage patterns to enhance features, fix bugs, and develop new functionality.
  • Communicate with You: Send administrative emails, support responses, and service updates.
  • Ensure Security: Detect and prevent fraud, unauthorized access, and other security threats.
  • Comply with Legal Obligations: Meet regulatory requirements and respond to lawful requests.

4. How We Share Your Information

4.1 With Your Employer

If you are an employee using ChronoWorks through your employer's account, your employer (the account administrator) has access to your time tracking data, location data (if GPS is enabled), schedule information, and profile information. This data is used by your employer for payroll, scheduling, and workforce management purposes.

4.2 Service Providers

We share information with third-party service providers who perform services on our behalf:

  • Firebase (Google Cloud): Database hosting, authentication, and cloud storage.
  • Google Maps API: Geolocation services and address verification.
  • SendGrid: Email delivery for notifications and support communications.
  • Payment Processors: Secure payment processing for subscription billing (if applicable).

4.3 Legal Requirements

We may disclose your information if required to do so by law or in response to valid requests by public authorities (e.g., court orders, subpoenas, or government agencies).

4.4 Business Transfers

If ChronoWorks is involved in a merger, acquisition, or asset sale, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction.

4.5 With Your Consent

We may share your information for any other purpose with your explicit consent.

5. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures to protect your information against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These measures include:

  • Encryption of data in transit and at rest
  • Secure authentication and access controls
  • Regular security assessments and updates
  • Firestore security rules to prevent unauthorized data access
  • Multi-tenant architecture ensuring data isolation between companies

However, no method of transmission over the Internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your information, we cannot guarantee its absolute security.

6. Your Data Rights

6.1 Access and Portability

You have the right to access your personal information and receive a copy of your data in a structured, commonly used format.

6.2 Correction

You can update your profile information directly in the app or by contacting your employer's account administrator.

6.3 Deletion

You may request deletion of your personal information. Note that your employer may be required to retain certain time tracking records for legal and payroll purposes.

6.4 Opt-Out of Location Tracking

You can disable location permissions in your device settings at any time. However, this may limit certain features like GPS-verified clock in/out.

6.5 Marketing Communications

You can opt out of receiving promotional emails by following the unsubscribe link in those emails.

6.6 Do Not Sell My Personal Information

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for their marketing purposes.

7. Data Retention

We retain your personal information for as long as necessary to provide the Service and fulfill the purposes outlined in this Privacy Policy. Time tracking records may be retained for up to 7 years to comply with employment laws and tax regulations, even after account termination.

Location data may be retained as long as the associated time entry exists, unless you request deletion and your employer has no legal requirement to retain it.

8. Children's Privacy

ChronoWorks is not intended for use by individuals under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 18. If we become aware that we have collected personal information from a child under 18, we will take steps to delete that information.

9. International Data Transfers

Your information may be transferred to and processed in countries other than your country of residence. These countries may have data protection laws that are different from your country's laws. We take steps to ensure that your information receives an adequate level of protection in the jurisdictions in which we process it.

10. California Privacy Rights (CCPA)

If you are a California resident, you have specific rights regarding your personal information under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA):

  • Right to know what personal information is collected, used, shared, or sold
  • Right to delete personal information held by us
  • Right to opt-out of the sale of personal information (note: we do not sell personal information)
  • Right to non-discrimination for exercising your CCPA rights

To exercise these rights, please contact us using the information provided below.

11. European Privacy Rights (GDPR)

If you are in the European Economic Area (EEA), you have certain data protection rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR):

  • Right to access, update, or delete your personal information
  • Right to rectification if your information is inaccurate or incomplete
  • Right to object to or restrict processing of your personal information
  • Right to data portability
  • Right to withdraw consent at any time
  • Right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority

12. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Privacy Policy on this page and updating the "Last Updated" date. You are advised to review this Privacy Policy periodically for any changes.

Continued use of the Service after changes are posted constitutes your acceptance of the updated Privacy Policy.

13. Third-Party Links

The Service may contain links to third-party websites or services. We are not responsible for the privacy practices or content of these third parties. We encourage you to read the privacy policies of any third-party sites you visit.
